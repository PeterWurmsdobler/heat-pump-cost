# Quantitative Analysis of Dynamic Heat Pump Operation for Domestic Heating

The preceding story, [How the Spark Gap Drives Radiator Upgrades for Heat Pump Installations ](https://medium.com/@peter-wurmsdobler/how-the-spark-gap-drives-radiator-upgrades-for-heat-pump-installations-1d3b098b29fd), addresses the implications of the spark gap on capital and operational expenses due to a change in radiator capacity through a simple static analysis, i.e. by looking at average quantities for heat loss and temperature for a heat pump in stationary operation. The situation changes if dynamic aspects are taken into account for the operation of the heat pump, setpoint temperature profiles and electricity tariffs. As presented in sources such as ["So You're Thinking About a Heat Pump: The UK Homeowner's Guide to Heat Pumps"](https://www.amazon.co.uk/Youre-Thinking-About-Heat-Pump/dp/B0GK7H511K/), heat pumps require therefore different operating strategies compared to gas boilers. The objective of this analysis is to demonstrate quantitatively how control and operation strategy impacts the economics of domestic heat pump heating.
